BeatMapSynth

Automatic Song Mapper for the VR Rhythm Game, Beat Saber

Beat Saber is a wildly popular VR rhythm game similar to Dance Dance Revolution or Guitar Hero. While the officially released songs are seen as the gold standard for quality play, the desire for new content outstrips the rate of release of official content. In response, users have developed their own custom content, often hosted on websites such as Beat Saver. This custom content varies greatly in quality, and furthermore, is difficult for an ameteur user to create on their own. I created BeatMapSynth in an effort to enable users to quickly and reliably create their own custom content using generative modeling techniques.

Using over 8,000 highly-rated, user-made song mappings downloaded and processed with a custom Python script, I trained Hidden Markov Models to produce sequences of block placements that replicate the smooth transitions similar to human-made maps. By adding custom-built programming to the generative model that incorporates analyzed features of the music (e.g., relative loudness and song structure), I was able to recreate the repetition and variation in block appearance rate that are common in human-made maps. These custom maps can be rapidly created, played as-is, or used as the building blocks with manual map editors.

Tanzanian Water Well Functionality

Machine learning to predict functionality of water well pumps for prioritizing repair and maintenance

Easy access to clean water is paramount to healthy communities. In order to identify water well pumps that may need updates or repairs, we created a model that predicts pump functionality in communities across Tanzania.

Using records for over 50,000 Tanzanian water wells from the agency Taarifa, which aggregates the data from the Tanzania Ministry of Water, and hosted at drivendata.org, we trained four independent classification models: Logistic Regression, K-Nearest Neighbor, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosted Forest. Individually, they performed well, ~80% accuracy, but we implemented a consensus method of the four models using Soft Voting to increase the accuracy to ~82% and diminish the effects of biases in the individual models.

Update on Opportunity Youth in South King Co.

Analysis of Public Use Microdata Samples (PUMS) to Track Unemployed, Unenrolled 16-24 Year Olds

Using 2017 US Census PUMS data, we provided an update to the estimate of "opportunity youth," 16 to 24 year olds who are neither employed nor enrolled in school, in South King Co., Washington. We compared these results to summarized 2014 data published here.

We found the total number of opportunity youth in the defined region had decreased since the 2014 data, as well as a total decrease in the proportion of opportunity youth in the community. In addition, we reported that the proportion of opportunity youth without college experience had increased in comparison to those with some college experience, suggesting that previous opportunity youth were able to find employment because of their college experience.
